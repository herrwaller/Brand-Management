第一节

What is a Brand?

什么是品牌？

Brands are everywhere — on every product we buy, every service we use, every institution we interact with. But what exactly is a brand? At its core, a brand is not the logo, the packaging, or the advertising campaign. A brand exists in the mind of the consumer. It is the sum of associations, feelings and expectations that a person connects with a name, symbol, or design.

The AMA (American Marketing Association) offers a formal definition: "A brand is a name, term, sign, symbol or design, or a combination of these, intended to identify the goods or services of one seller or group of sellers, and to differentiate them from those of a competitor." More precisely, brands are "images in the minds of stakeholders that assume an identification and differentiation function and shape choice processes" (Esch, 2014). A brand is a set of mental associations held by the consumer which add to the perceived value of a product or service.

品牌无处不在——存在于我们购买的每一件产品、使用的每一项服务、接触的每一个机构之中。但品牌究竟是什么？从根本上说，品牌不是标志、包装或广告活动，而是存在于消费者心智中的东西。它是一个人与某一名称、符号或设计相关联的印象、情感与期待的总和。

美国营销协会（AMA）给出了正式定义："品牌是名称、术语、标志、符号或设计，或其组合，旨在识别一个或一组销售者的商品或服务，并将其与竞争对手区分开来。"更深刻地说，品牌是"存在于利益相关者心智中的形象，承担着识别与差异化功能，并塑造购买决策过程"（Esch，2014）。品牌是消费者持有的一组心理联想，这些联想为产品或服务增加感知价值。

"Products are made in the factory, but brands are created in the mind." — Walter Landor

产品在工厂里制造，但品牌在心智中创造。——沃尔特·兰多

第二节

What Can Be Branded?

什么可以被品牌化？

The scope of branding extends far beyond consumer products. Virtually anything that creates associations and value in people's minds can function as a brand. The branding of universities is a powerful example: Harvard's brand communicates prestige, intellectual rigour and global network access — and people pay premium prices (via tuition fees) for access to that brand.

品牌化的范围远超消费品。几乎任何能在人们心中建立联想与价值的事物都可以成为品牌。大学品牌化是一个有力的案例：哈佛的品牌传达了声誉、学术严谨性和全球校友网络——人们为此支付溢价（通过学费）以获得品牌准入。

第三节

Products vs. Brands

产品与品牌

There is a fundamental distinction between a product and a brand. A product is what a company makes; a brand is what the customer buys. Many products claim to be brands but are not: they are "me-too" products with poor positioning, poor quality and poor support. What separates true brands from products is the depth and consistency of associations they build.

The Pepsi Challenge of 1975/1983 illustrated this with striking clarity: In blind taste tests, consumers preferred Pepsi over Coca-Cola. But in open tests, with the brand visible, Coke won decisively. The brand — its history, identity, emotional associations — overrode taste perception. Brand value is thus not just functional; it is psychological, cultural, and social. Strong brands justify premium pricing, drive loyalty, and serve as a platform for innovation. A Porsche Cayenne and a VW Touareg share the same platform — yet the Porsche commands a dramatically higher price purely on the basis of brand perception.

产品与品牌之间存在根本区别。产品是公司制造的东西；品牌是顾客购买的东西。许多产品声称自己是品牌，但实际上只是"跟风产品"——定位模糊、质量平庸、缺乏支撑。将真正品牌与普通产品区分开来的，是它们所构建的联想的深度和一致性。

1975/1983年的百事挑战赛清晰地说明了这一点：在盲测中，消费者更偏爱百事可乐而非可口可乐。但在展示品牌标识的开放测试中，可口可乐以绝对优势胜出。品牌——其历史、身份与情感联想——凌驾于口味感知之上。品牌价值因此不仅仅是功能性的，更是心理的、文化的和社会的。保时捷卡宴与大众途锐共用同一平台——但保时捷仅凭品牌感知就能获得远高于对方的价格溢价。

Product / 产品
  • Functional object
  • Generic associations
  • Replaceable
  • Price-sensitive
Brand / 品牌
  • Mental image in consumer's mind
  • Unique emotional associations
  • Loyal following
  • Premium pricing possible
Section 4
第四节

What Strong Brands Can Do

强大品牌能做什么

A strong brand is one of the most valuable assets a company can possess — often exceeding the value of its physical assets. In 2024, the world's most valuable brands (Apple, Google, Amazon) are worth hundreds of billions of dollars in brand equity alone.

强大的品牌是企业最有价值的资产之一——其价值往往超过有形资产。2024年，全球最有价值的品牌（苹果、谷歌、亚马逊）仅品牌资产价值就达数千亿美元。

Benefit 01
优势 01
Premium Pricing
溢价定价
The same basic product (e.g., butter biscuits: Leibniz vs. store-brand) can sell at dramatically different price points when branded effectively.
同样的基础产品（如黄油饼干：Leibniz与自有品牌）在有效品牌化后可以实现截然不同的价格定位。
Benefit 02
优势 02
Build Loyalty
建立忠诚度
Consumers return repeatedly, reducing customer acquisition costs. Loyal customers are the backbone of long-term brand profitability.
消费者反复回购，降低客户获取成本。忠实顾客是品牌长期盈利的基础。
Benefit 03
优势 03
New Product Launches
促进新产品推出
A trusted brand name reduces perceived risk for new products, making market entry faster and more cost-effective.
可信的品牌名称降低新产品的感知风险，使市场进入更快、更具成本效益。
Benefit 04
优势 04
Crisis Resilience
危机抵御力
Strong brands recover faster from reputational damage than weak ones. Brand equity acts as a buffer during crises.
强大品牌比弱势品牌更快从声誉损害中恢复。品牌资产在危机中起到缓冲作用。
Benefit 05
优势 05
Attract Talent & Partners
吸引人才与合作伙伴
Strong employer brands attract better employees; strong product brands attract better retail placement and distribution partners.
强大的雇主品牌吸引更优秀的员工；强大的产品品牌获得更好的零售陈列位置和分销合作伙伴。
Benefit 06
优势 06
Balance Sheet Value
资产负债表价值
Brands are intangible assets that appear on balance sheets as goodwill. Apple, Google, and Amazon brands are valued at hundreds of billions.
品牌是以商誉形式出现在资产负债表上的无形资产。苹果、谷歌和亚马逊品牌价值数千亿美元。
Assignment 1
Develop your Brand Brief
制定你的品牌简介

Choose a brand you would like to analyse throughout this semester — a product brand, service brand, or personal brand. The brand should be one you find interesting and for which you can find sufficient information. Write a brief brand overview (1–2 slides) covering:

  • (1) What is the brand? (name, category, origin)
  • (2) What does it sell / offer?
  • (3) Who are the main target groups?
  • (4) Why did you choose this brand?

选择一个你希望在本学期深入分析的品牌——可以是产品品牌、服务品牌或个人品牌。这个品牌应该让你感兴趣，并且能够找到足够的信息。撰写一份简短的品牌概览（1-2页幻灯片），内容涵盖：

  • （1）这是什么品牌？（名称、品类、起源）
  • （2）它销售/提供什么？
  • （3）主要目标群体是谁？
  • （4）你为何选择这个品牌？
