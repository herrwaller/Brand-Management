第一节

Why Go International?

为何国际化？

In the digital era, going international is no longer optional for growing brands — it often happens before companies plan for it. Orders arrive from foreign markets via e-commerce, and brands find themselves with international audiences before they have an international strategy.

Once foreign sales account for a significant share of revenues, deliberate brand management becomes essential. Beyond revenue growth, internationalisation involves complex strategic challenges: How do we protect our brand in new markets? Should we standardise our approach or adapt to local preferences? How do we navigate cultural, legal, and competitive differences?

The overarching tension is between the efficiency of standardisation (one consistent brand globally) and the effectiveness of localisation (a brand adapted to resonate with local consumers). Neither extreme works for most brands — most effective strategies are adaptive hybrids.

在数字时代，国际化对于成长中的品牌来说不再是可选项——它往往在企业计划之前就已发生。通过电子商务，来自海外市场的订单不期而至，品牌在制定国际化战略之前就已拥有了国际受众。

一旦海外销售占收入的重要份额，有意识的品牌管理就变得至关重要。除了收入增长，国际化还涉及复杂的战略挑战：我们如何在新市场保护我们的品牌？我们应该标准化我们的方式还是适应当地偏好？我们如何应对文化、法律和竞争差异？

首要张力在于标准化的效率（全球一致的品牌）与本地化的有效性（适应与当地消费者产生共鸣的品牌）之间。对于大多数品牌来说，两个极端都行不通——最有效的战略是自适应混合策略。

PESTLE Analysis: Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental — the macro-environmental factors a brand must assess before entering any new market.
PESTLE分析：政治、经济、社会、技术、法律、环境——品牌进入任何新市场前必须评估的宏观环境因素。

From Local to Global

从本土到全球

Internationalisation rarely happens in one leap. Brands typically expand in concentric stages — first to nearby markets with similar languages or legal systems, then to culturally adjacent regions, and finally to distant global markets. Each expansion stage multiplies complexity: more languages, more regulations, more competitors, more cultural distance from the brand's origin.

The slider below traces one hypothetical expansion path for a brand headquartered in Ningbo, China — a major manufacturing and export hub — showing how geographic reach, market complexity, and brand adaptation requirements change at each stage.

国际化很少在一步内完成。品牌通常以同心圆的阶段扩张——首先进入语言或法律体系相似的邻近市场，然后是文化相近的地区，最后才是遥远的全球市场。每个扩张阶段都会倍增复杂性：更多语言、更多法规、更多竞争对手、与品牌起源地更大的文化距离。

第三节

PESTEL Analysis

PESTEL分析

Before entering a new market, a brand must map the macro-environmental forces that will shape its opportunity. PESTEL analysis structures this scan across six dimensions — Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, Legal — turning an overwhelming complexity into a manageable strategic framework.

The case study below applies a full PESTEL to a scenario that is strategically plausible but not yet real: Xiaomi Car (小米汽车) entering the German market. Xiaomi launched its SU7 electric sedan in China in 2024 but has not entered Europe. Germany — Europe's largest car market and home to BMW, Mercedes-Benz, and Volkswagen — represents the ultimate test for any foreign automotive brand.

在进入新市场之前，品牌必须梳理将影响其机会的宏观环境力量。PESTEL分析将这种扫描结构化为六个维度——政治、经济、社会、技术、环境、法律——将压倒性的复杂性转化为可管理的战略框架。

下方的案例研究将完整的PESTEL应用于一个战略上可行但尚未实现的场景：小米汽车进入德国市场。小米于2024年在中国推出SU7电动轿车，但尚未进入欧洲。德国——欧洲最大汽车市场，宝马、奔驰和大众的故乡——对任何外国汽车品牌来说都是终极考验。

第四节

Counterfeits

仿冒品

Global losses due to counterfeiting exceed $20 billion annually. Counterfeits — fake products designed to mislead consumers into believing they are authentic — operate at all levels of the economy and in virtually all product categories. They are not simply a nuisance; they erode brand equity, undermine quality control, and can cause physical harm (fake pharmaceuticals, fraudulent safety equipment).

Particularly in China, counterfeiting was historically systemic — brands such as Boy London found their products already being counterfeited before they officially entered the Chinese market.

Strategies to combat counterfeits: "Search & Destroy" — hiring private investigation agencies to track down fakes. Technical encoding — unique micro-signatures in fabrics, materials, or packaging (e.g., Levi's micro-weave patterns). Legal action — though complex and slow in cross-border contexts. Digital authentication — QR codes, blockchain-based provenance tracking.

The best long-term protection is a brand that is so deeply associated with quality, craftsmanship, and authenticity that consumers can distinguish the real product from imitations — and are motivated to do so.

全球每年因仿冒品造成的损失超过200亿美元。仿冒品——设计用来误导消费者相信其为正品的假冒产品——在经济的各个层面和几乎所有产品品类中运作。它们不仅仅是麻烦；它们侵蚀品牌资产、破坏质量控制，并可能造成人身伤害（假冒药品、欺诈性安全设备）。

特别是在中国，仿冒在历史上是系统性的——像Boy London这样的品牌发现其产品在正式进入中国市场之前就已经被仿冒。

打击仿冒品的策略："搜索与销毁"——雇用私人调查机构追踪假货。技术编码——织物、材料或包装中的独特微签名（如李维斯微织纹）。法律行动——尽管在跨境背景下复杂而缓慢。数字认证——二维码、基于区块链的溯源追踪。

最好的长期保护是一个与质量、工艺和真实性如此深度关联的品牌，使消费者能够区分真品与仿品——并有动力这样做。

Standardise or Adapt?

标准化还是适应？

The fundamental strategic question in internationalisation is whether to standardise brand elements across all markets or adapt them to local conditions.

国际化中的基本战略问题是在所有市场标准化品牌要素还是适应当地条件。

Standardised Approach 标准化方法
📱 Apple — "Shot on iPhone" 📱 苹果 — "用iPhone拍摄"
2015 → ongoing · 70+ countries · identical creative 2015年至今 · 70+国家 · 相同创意

The same user-generated photographs appear on billboards in Tokyo, New York, Berlin, and São Paulo — no translated tagline, no market-specific messaging. Only the photographer's home city changes. The creative concept, visual style, and brand promise are identical worldwide.

相同的用户拍摄照片出现在东京、纽约、柏林和圣保罗的广告牌上——无翻译标语，无特定市场信息。只有摄影师的所在城市不同。创意概念、视觉风格和品牌承诺在全球完全一致。

India's Hindu majority does not eat beef; roughly 40% of the population is vegetarian. McDonald's replaced its signature beef patties with spiced potato-and-pea patties, created a fully separate vegetarian menu, and reframed its value proposition around Indian taste preferences. The golden arches stayed — almost everything else changed.

印度印度教大多数人不吃牛肉；约40%的人口是素食者。麦当劳将其标志性牛肉饼换成了香料土豆豌豆饼，创建了完全独立的素食菜单，并围绕印度口味偏好重新定位其价值主张。金色拱门保留了——几乎其他一切都变了。

In practice, the "glocal" approach dominates among successful international brands: standardise the brand core (identity, values, visual system) but adapt the execution (messaging, product variants, media channels, local references). McDonald's is the classic example: the yellow arches and "I'm lovin' it" are global; the menu varies dramatically by market (McArabia, McSpicy in Asia, Teriyaki Burger in Japan).

在实践中，"全球本土化"方法在成功的国际品牌中占主导地位：标准化品牌核心（识别、价值观、视觉系统）但调整执行（信息、产品变体、媒体渠道、本地参考）。麦当劳是经典案例：黄色拱门和"我就喜欢"是全球性的；菜单因市场而大相径庭（麦阿拉伯、麦辣在亚洲、照烧汉堡在日本）。

第六节

Three Adaptation Areas

三个适应领域

When entering new markets, brands may need to adapt across three main areas. Famous name failures include "Nothing sucks like an Electrolux" (unfortunate American slang), "Gros Mec" for McDonald's Big Mac in France (meant "Big Pimp"), and Orange telecom's slogan in Northern Ireland (where "Orange" implied Protestant/loyalist associations). In China, Western brands create Mandarin transliterations that must be phonetically similar AND carry positive connotations: Coca-Cola → 可口可乐 (Kěkǒu Kělè = "delicious happiness").

进入新市场时，品牌可能需要在三个主要领域进行调整。著名的名称失败案例包括："Nothing sucks like an Electrolux"（不幸的美式俚语）、麦当劳Big Mac在法国称为"Gros Mec"（在法语俚语中意为"大皮条客"），以及橙色电信在北爱尔兰的口号（"橙色"暗示新教/保皇派联想）。在中国，西方品牌创建普通话音译，必须在音韵上相似并带有积极内涵：Coca-Cola → 可口可乐（Kěkǒu Kělè = "美味的幸福"）。

01

Name & Logo Adaptation

名称与标志适应

The brand name and logo must be trademark-free and carry acceptable, ideally positive connotations in the local language and culture.

品牌名称和标志必须（商标上）可用，并在当地语言和文化中具有可接受的、理想上是积极的内涵。

02

Product Adaptation

产品适应

Local tastes, incomes, infrastructure, and regulations may force product changes. VW Terramont: a supersized SUV developed exclusively for China.

当地口味、收入、基础设施和法规可能迫使产品变更。大众途锐：一款专为中国开发的超大型SUV。

03

Communication Adaptation

传播适应

Language and cultural differences almost always require communication adaptation, even when the brand strategy is standardised.

语言和文化差异几乎总是需要传播适应，即使品牌战略是标准化的。

第七节

Case Studies: Expansion Successes & Failures

案例研究：扩张的成功与失败

International expansion is not guaranteed to succeed — even for powerful global brands. Two contrasting stories illustrate the key dynamics: a brand acquisition turned retention battle, and a coffee giant that succeeded in one Asian market by failing in another.

国际扩张并不保证成功——即使对于强大的全球品牌也不例外。两个对比鲜明的故事说明了关键动态：一次品牌收购演变成保留大战，以及一家咖啡巨头在一个亚洲市场成功、在另一个市场失败的经历。

01

Coca-Cola Acquires Thums Up — And Almost Loses

可口可乐收购Thums Up——险些得不偿失

When Coca-Cola entered India in 1993 by acquiring Thums Up — then the dominant soft drink with 85% market share and a strong "macho" brand identity — its strategy was to gradually phase out Thums Up and replace it with Coca-Cola. Marketing budgets were cut; distribution channels were stocked with Coke instead. But Thums Up's loyal consumers, especially young men, rejected the "nicer" Coca-Cola image. Instead of switching to Coke, they switched to Pepsi. Coca-Cola eventually reversed course, investing heavily in Thums Up — and today the brand outperforms Coca-Cola in many Indian states. Lesson: strong local brand equity can outlast the acquirer's ambitions.

当可口可乐于1993年通过收购Thums Up进入印度市场时，Thums Up当时占据主导地位，市场份额高达85%，拥有强烈的"硬汉"品牌形象。可口可乐的策略是逐步淘汰Thums Up并用可口可乐取代它。营销预算被削减；分销渠道被换成可口可乐。但Thums Up的忠实消费者，尤其是年轻男性，拒绝接受"更温和"的可口可乐形象。他们没有转向可口可乐，而是转向了百事可乐。可口可乐最终逆转策略，大力投资Thums Up——如今，该品牌在印度许多邦的表现优于可口可乐。教训：强大的本地品牌资产可以超越收购方的野心。

02

Starbucks: Success in China, Failure in Australia

星巴克：在中国成功，在澳大利亚失败

Starbucks succeeded in China partly because it introduced European coffee culture to a market that had no established café tradition — there was pent-up demand, and Starbucks owned the category. In Australia, the opposite was true: Australia already had a sophisticated, independent café culture (flat whites, single-origin espresso bars) when Starbucks arrived. Consumers found Starbucks overpriced and inauthentic compared to local alternatives. Starbucks closed 61 of its 84 Australian stores in 2008. The lesson: global brand success depends on the presence — or absence — of local competition and existing consumer habits. The same product can be a novelty in one market and a disappointment in another.

星巴克在中国的成功部分是因为它将欧洲咖啡文化引入了一个没有成熟咖啡馆传统的市场——存在被压抑的需求，星巴克占据了这一品类。在澳大利亚，情况恰恰相反：当星巴克进入时，澳大利亚已经拥有成熟的独立咖啡文化（澳白咖啡、单品咖啡馆）。与当地替代品相比，消费者认为星巴克定价过高且缺乏真实性。2008年，星巴克关闭了其澳大利亚84家门店中的61家。教训：全球品牌的成功取决于本地竞争和现有消费者习惯的存在——或缺失。同一产品在一个市场可能是新奇事物，在另一个市场则可能令人失望。

03

Pattern Standardization: MasterCard's "Priceless" Campaign

模式标准化：万事达卡"无价"活动

MasterCard's "Priceless" campaign is a textbook example of pattern standardization — using the same storyline and emotional appeal across markets while adapting the specific context, actors, and cultural references. The core message ("There are some things money can't buy — for everything else, there's MasterCard") remained constant in every country, but the scenarios were locally produced: a family outing in India, a football match in Brazil, a weekend trip in Germany. This approach kept cost efficiency while making the campaign feel local and relevant everywhere. Pattern standardization offers a middle path between full standardisation (cost-efficient but culturally distant) and full adaptation (highly relevant but expensive).

万事达卡的"无价"活动是模式标准化的教科书案例——在不同市场使用相同的故事线和情感诉求，同时调整具体场景、演员和文化参考。核心信息（"有些东西是金钱买不到的——其他一切，万事达卡都可以"）在每个国家保持不变，但场景是本地制作的：印度的家庭郊游、巴西的足球比赛、德国的周末旅行。这种方法保持了成本效率，同时使活动在各地感觉本地化且相关。模式标准化提供了完全标准化（成本高效但文化距离远）和完全适应化（高度相关但昂贵）之间的中间道路。

第八节

Global Branding — Criticism & Limits

全球品牌化——批评与局限

The rise of global brands has not gone uncontested. Critics argue that global branding homogenises cultures, exploits workers in developing countries, and concentrates power in the hands of a few multinational corporations. The most influential critique came from Canadian author Naomi Klein in her 2000 book No Logo — which became a manifesto of the anti-globalisation movement.

Klein argued that global corporations had shifted from producing goods to producing brands — outsourcing manufacturing to the cheapest locations while investing in brand image at home. The result: enormous brand premiums paid by consumers, low wages for factory workers, and growing corporate control over public space and culture.

Beyond ideology, there are also structural limits to global brand dominance. As emerging markets grow wealthier, local consumers develop stronger preferences for locally relevant brands. Research suggests that as incomes rise, consumers in markets like China, India, and Brazil increasingly favour local brands that understand their culture over foreign brands that feel generic. Global brands that fail to localise risk becoming irrelevant as markets mature.

全球品牌的崛起并未得到普遍认可。批评者认为，全球品牌化同质化了文化，剥削了发展中国家的工人，并将权力集中在少数跨国公司手中。最具影响力的批评来自加拿大作者娜奥米·克莱因，她在2000年的著作《No Logo》中——成为反全球化运动的宣言。

克莱因认为，全球企业已从生产商品转变为生产品牌——将制造业外包给成本最低的地方，同时在国内投资品牌形象。结果：消费者支付了巨额品牌溢价，工厂工人获得低工资，企业对公共空间和文化的控制日益增强。

除了意识形态之外，全球品牌主导地位也存在结构性局限。随着新兴市场变得更加富裕，本地消费者对本土相关品牌的偏好越来越强。研究表明，随着收入增长，中国、印度和巴西等市场的消费者越来越倾向于了解其文化的本地品牌，而不是感觉通用的外国品牌。未能本地化的全球品牌可能会随着市场成熟而变得无关紧要。

"A global brand that stops adapting is a brand that starts declining." — Expansion without localisation creates reach but destroys relevance.
"停止适应的全球品牌就是开始衰落的品牌。"——没有本地化的扩张创造了覆盖范围，但破坏了相关性。
